# Hard Targets & Attached Forces

An optional method to further raise the stakes is by introducing **Hard Targets**. These can be mobile armored machines such as tanks, or entrenched static positions such as a gun nest.

Hard Targets must be targeted separately during the [[Fire]] action. Some equipment, such as rocket launchers, provide a clear and distinct advantage against Hard Targets.

When Firing upon a Hard Target, set those d6 aside in a separate pool. When rolling during the [[Offense Roll]], a **6** counts as a Hit, and a **4–5** as a Hit at a Cost. A Hit at a Cost works the same as Success at a Cost, dealing 1 Hit of damage but giving one Trooper **-1 DEF** during the Defense Roll. You can choose to not take the Hit at a Cost.

Interpret/reflavor Hard Targets at will; a Brute can be a bipedal armored walker, a hulking monstrosity, or just a very big guy with a very big gun.

## Attached Forces

The Squad can be accompanied by additional fighters, escorts, or hangers-on. Assign them a bonus based on rough size: **+1 ATK** for a few, **+2 ATK** for a sizable group, **+3 ATK** for a large one.

Attached forces don't participate in Advance Rolls, hanging back to let the Squad make their move. 

During any Exchange, the Squad can choose to commit them to the Offense Roll. Roll their dice alongside but keep them separate. Any die that comes up 1 is removed - representing casualties. When all dice are removed, the attached force is gone.

**VIPs**. When escorting a VIP, the VIP is always the last die in the group. They can't be targeted separately, but if committed to an Exchange and the last die rolls a 1, the VIP is killed.
