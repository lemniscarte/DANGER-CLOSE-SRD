# The Squad

A [[Squad]] consists of **5 [[Trooper|Troopers]]**. At the beginning of the game, name the Squad and the Troopers. One of your Troopers, usually the first Trooper on your sheet, is the Squad's **Sergeant**. 

A Trooper has the following variables to keep track of. Most of these only come into play during [[Engagement|Engagements]], but some affect the [[Mission Phase]] as well.

## Status

Current level of health of the Trooper. Ranges from [[OK]] to [[Grazed]] to [[Wounded]] to [[Bleeding Out]] to [[Dead]].

## Gear

A Trooper's [[Armor]], [[Weapon]] and [[Special Gear]], such as [[Special Weapons]] and [[Special Equipment]].

## Grit

An expendable resource that allows a Trooper to push through. Can be used to re-roll a [[Movement|Move]] or [[Defense Roll]], as well as used for special skills. If a Trooper survives a Mission, they replenish their Grit and gain an extra use, to a maximum of 3.

## Ammo

An abstraction of munitions and supplies carried. A Trooper starts a mission with 3 Ammo, and can hold a maximum of 3. It can be expended to fire [[Special Weapons]] and enhance attacks. Can be replenished by finding Ammo in the field.

## Mobility 

The Trooper's ability to maneuver and haul ass. Base value is **5**, lowered by selected armor, weapons & gear. This is the target number for all [[Mobility Check|Mobility Checks]] (roll 1d6 equal or under). Mobility also affects a Trooper's [[Flanking Bonus]].

## Tags

To aid in roleplay and improvisation, give each Trooper a Tag. Their Tag describes how they approach problems and engage non-combat situations.

- **Forceful**. Physical solutions. Kicks doors, carries wounded, holds the line through brute effort. Intimidates when talking fails.
- **Technical**. Systems and logic. Hacks terminals, disarms devices, reads schematics, operates unfamiliar equipment.
- **Steady**. Composure and patience. Waits out a tense situation, resists interrogation, holds position for hours, talks down a panicking civilian.
- **Sharp**. Reads people and situations. Notices the detail others miss, fast-talks past a checkpoint, picks up that something is wrong before anyone else does.

> [!tip] First game?
> Keep your Squad nice and simple: come up with 5 Trooper names and give them each an **Assault Rifle**, **Medium Armor** (Mobility 4, Flanking Bonus +2 ATK), 1 Grit, 3 Ammo. Play a simple Mission with a single Sector ([[Dense Cover]], [[Open Space]], [[Threat Level]] 1), and jump ahead to the [[Mission Phase]] to start!
